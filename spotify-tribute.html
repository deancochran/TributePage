Intro to Spotify's Recommendation System

Launched in 2008, Spotify is a Swedish start-up audio streaming platform that offers tier subscription services, earning revenue by means of advertising and premium subscription fees. If you use Spotify, they have beeen utilizing complex algorithms to generalize your peresonality, and your taste in music eveer sincee you signed up.

They log every interaction you have in their web page and keep the data gathered to make a respenstation of yourself during the time spent on the website under your account name. If you don't use Spotify, you must have a good reason. As of now they have lowered their subscription fees and even offeered users a more personalized experience with their music. Spotify's customers find real value in the provided serevice, and it is all centered around data science.

Spotify doesn't just keep track of which songs you listen to and when, but they form analyses on implict data to form reasons for why you may or may not want to listen to a particular song.

This is what sets apart Spotify from the rest of its competitors like Apple Music, Amazon Prime Music, and Google Music who hire entities who create playlists and identify similar sounds bsed off a trend. Spotify launched its Discover Weekly playlist back in 2015. It ws and still is an algorithm generated playlist that is updated every Monday. This sole feature of Spotify has been bringing millions of listeners hours of custom, curated music recommendations everey week. Spotify also offers other customized recommendations in Daily Mixes, Release Radar and Recommended suggestions of playlists or artists. According to Spotify Discover Weekly had reached nearly 5 billion tracks streamed since its launch in 2015, a clear sign of its success.

Users claimed it was scary how well Spotify was able to discern their musical tastes and that the platform knew them.
